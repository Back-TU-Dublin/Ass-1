Balance speed and resource use based on the task’s needs.Simplified Reflection on Maze-Solving Without Sensors​The core challenge lies in balancing algorithm efficiency and adaptability. Basic methods like DFS risk inefficiency, while BFS or A* ensure completeness and optimality but require more memory. For dynamic obstacles or unknown maze changes, hybrid approaches (e.g., combining pre-mapping with real-time re-planning) improve robustness. Energy efficiency matters; optimizing movement patterns and simplifying decision-making logic reduces computational strain. Avoiding over-reliance on rigid rules (e.g., "right-hand rule") allows better handling of complex branches. Ultimately, success depends on tailoring algorithms to the task’s constraints—simplicity for speed, complexity for adaptability.
